Wildfire smoke health tracker

Tracking current PM₂.₅ exposure across Ontario, Alberta, and British Columbia, and the estimated health consequences of wildfire smoke.
July 23, 2026 Updated 6:50 a.m. EDT 38 of 43 stations reporting Dave Sawyer · Canadian Climate Institute
For more detail on methodology see: The health impacts of Canada's new, smoky summer reality
Sources and method: Ontario: Air Quality Ontario PM₂.₅ station data (pollutant code 124), automatically polled, preliminary until final verification. Alberta: Alberta Environment and Protected Areas OData API, Fine Particulate Matter (ParameterKey 62), real-time PM₂.₅ µg/m³. British Columbia: BC Ministry of Environment and Parks hourly PM₂.₅ CSV (bc-env-aq network, 60+ stations), updated hourly, preliminary until annual verification. Industrial point source stations excluded. Readings are the simple average across reporting stations. Health impacts draw on the CCI wildfire health model and AQBAT v3.0, using a clean air baseline of 6 µg/m³ and values in 2023 Canadian dollars. CCI wildfire health model, 2014 to 2024. Matz et al. 2026.
